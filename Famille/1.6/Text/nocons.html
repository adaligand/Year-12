Due to the complexity of relationships in a blended family, it is often difficult for children to adapt to a new situation. Indeed, children may feel excluded when they have to share their parent with a new partner, especially if step-siblings are involved. Consequently, creating a new family unit involves managing relational problems and arbitrating jealousies, which is essential to avoid the accumulation of resentments. It is crucial to be attentive to the children's potential concerns to create the conditions for harmony. Furthermore, it is important to resolve painful conflicts and help children find their place and gain respect. Ultimately, the impact of the family breakup on the child is significant, as it affects not only the relationship with the present and absent parent but also the issue of roles and territories within the new extended family.

En outre, il est important de régler les conflits douloureux et d'aider les enfants à trouver leur place et à se faire respecter.
